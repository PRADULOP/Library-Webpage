"1984" by George Orwell

Set in a dystopian future where the world is divided into three totalitarian superstates, 1984 follows Winston Smith, a low-ranking member of the Party, who begins to question the oppressive regime of Big Brother. The novel explores themes of government surveillance, the manipulation of truth, and the loss of individuality. With its portrayal of a society where free thought is illegal and reality is controlled by the state, 1984 remains a chilling warning about the dangers of totalitarianism and the erosion of personal freedoms.
